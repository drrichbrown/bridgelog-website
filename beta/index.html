A personal note

A Brief Word from Rich

For years I've jotted bidding information on little slips of paper, and I've watched many players struggle to record contracts, opening leads, results—and sometimes even the auction itself—accurately on paper scoresheets.

My hope is that BridgeLog will make recording faster, easier, and more accurate. Even better, by keeping complete records of every auction, opening lead, result, and note, BridgeLog gives partners a powerful new way to review sessions, identify opportunities for improvement, and become better players.

Thank you sincerely for helping test BridgeLog.

Before you begin

Before You Play Your First Board

I tried to make BridgeLog as intuitive as possible, but there are a few things you should know before getting started.

1

Start a New Session

Select your partner and the game location from the lists. If your partner or location is not already listed, you can easily add it.

2

Choose Your Partnership Direction

Select whether you will play North and/or East, or South and/or West.

In a Howell movement, one player must play North and East, and the other must play South and West.

3

Follow Normal Duplicate Bridge Procedures

At the start of each board, confirm the board number and your partnership's direction. BridgeLog automatically determines the dealer and vulnerability. Enter the auction, opening lead, result, and any notes as play progresses.

4

Director-Approved Irregular Bids

If the director allows an irregular bid to stand, tap the gavel button before entering the bid. BridgeLog will record the bid while indicating that it was director-approved.

5

Earlier Boards

If the director asks about a previous board during the session, tap Boards to review any earlier board without interrupting your current session.

6

After the Session

When the session is over, tap Review Email to review your automatically generated session report. Then tap Send Email to email it to yourself and/or your partner.

7

Send Feedback

Please send at least one feedback email after you have used BridgeLog. Tell me what worked well, what did not work as expected, and how BridgeLog could be easier and better. Screenshots are especially helpful when reporting a problem.

Installation

Install BridgeLog through TestFlight

Apple distributes beta versions of iPhone apps through its free TestFlight app. Rich will add you as a BridgeLog tester, and Apple will then send a TestFlight invitation to the email address associated with your Apple ID. Complete these steps on the iPhone you will use for testing.

  1. Before installing BridgeLog, read the orientation and getting-started instructions on this page.
  2. Open the App Store and install TestFlight.
  3. Watch for a TestFlight invitation email from Apple.
  4. Open the invitation on your iPhone and tap View in TestFlight or Start Testing.
  5. In TestFlight, tap Accept if requested, then tap Install.
  6. Open BridgeLog from TestFlight or from your Home Screen.
  7. Return to this page whenever you need setup instructions, testing suggestions, or the feedback link.

If you do not receive Apple's invitation after Rich tells you it has been sent, check your spam or junk folder and confirm that Rich used the email address associated with your Apple ID.

First-time setup

Get ready for your first session

Initial setup should take only a few minutes. You can change these entries later.

1

Create your user profile

Enter your name, the short name you want displayed during play, your email address, and—optionally—your ACBL number.

2

Add a partner

Add the partner you will use for testing. BridgeLog uses the partner’s short name during play and in session reports.

3

Add a location or game

Add a useful description such as “Santa Barbara Bridge Center Open Game.” This will help you find the session later.

Testing priorities

What I would especially like you to test

Use BridgeLog naturally, but pay particular attention to the areas below.

During the auction

  • Confirm the board number and your position.
  • Enter ordinary bids, passes, doubles, and redoubles.
  • Try correcting a mistaken bid with Backspace.
  • Confirm that invalid bids are unavailable.

During and after play

  • Record the opening lead.
  • Enter made contracts, overtricks, and defeated contracts.
  • Correct an auction, lead, or result from the Summary screen.
  • Add a note to one or more boards.

After the session

  • Review earlier boards and previous sessions.
  • Check scores from your side’s perspective.
  • Open the session report.
  • Review email, PDF, and Excel report output.
